# A Laboratory for Play

![Loodo](/lab-notes/a-laboratory-for-play/loodo.png "wide")

In 2009, we started a small laboratory for play.

Fresh out of college, Caetano Borges, Thiago Camargo, and I created Loodo, a blog where we explored games from a different angle. Not just as players, but as people taking systems apart, trying to understand how they worked and what made them meaningful.

This was before algorithms shaped what people saw online. We followed curiosity wherever it led us. We built small experiments, shared ideas, and somehow, people noticed.
(still not sure how that happened)

Our work [was cited by authors we admired,](https://www.raphkoster.com/2009/02/13/atof-tetris-variant-comes-true/)"was cited by authors we admired"), [referenced in books](https://books.google.com.my/books?id=5-LIvgZcG-gC&printsec=frontcover&source=gbs_atb#v=onepage&q=Loodo&f=false "referenced in books"), and even [inspired fan-made versions of some of our games](http://https://steamcommunity.com/sharedfiles/filedetails/?id=485340291 "inspired fan-made versions of some of our games").

> The “dressing,” the art and especially the sound, was so strong that after a while, I couldn’t ignore it, and it made me uncomfortable. - Raph Koster

Eventually, life pulled us in different directions. We each stayed close to games in our own ways, but from a distance. Over time, Loodo came to an end.
(no dramatic ending, it just faded out)

Now, almost 20 years later, I’m returning to that idea, on purpose.

## New lab, same ideas.

Ludoratory is a laboratory for play. A space for experimenting with games, systems, and code. A place to explore what makes things fun, by building, testing, and breaking things along the way.

<NoteUp>lab + ludus. couldn’t resist</NoteUp>

This project wouldn’t exist without Loodo, without Caetano, Thiago, and Álvaro, who joined us along the way. So even though Ludoratory is officially starting now, it carries years of history behind it, and with that, a certain sense of responsibility.

If you’re here, you’re part of the experiment.

Play the games. Tinker with the code. Reach out.

The goal is simple: to understand fun by building it.
(and probably fail a lot along the way)
